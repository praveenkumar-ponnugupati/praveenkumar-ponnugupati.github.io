01 // about

About Me

I started out studying aerospace — spending three years obsessing over how aircraft wings fail, how drag coefficients behave at high Reynolds numbers, and why ancient temple domes are aerodynamically better than most modern buildings. That kind of engineering trains you to think about systems under pressure: where they break, and why.

When I graduated in 2021, aerospace hiring in India had collapsed. Instead of waiting, I took a role at Cognizant building production systems for Travelers Insurance. Turns out the instincts transferred perfectly — production engineering is just a different kind of systems thinking. I spent 3.5 years managing Red Hat infrastructure at scale, automating with Terraform and Python, shipping CI/CD pipelines, and learning what it actually takes to keep software running reliably for a major insurer. Most data scientists never get that exposure. I think in production, not just notebooks.

Now I'm at UConn doing an MS in AI & Machine Learning — and building the things I couldn't stop thinking about while managing servers. A local chess coaching agent that runs entirely on-device and remembers your playing patterns across sessions. A macOS daemon that silently tracks your real typing mistakes and trains you to stop repeating them. The through-line from aerospace to Cognizant to here is the same: I want to understand how complex systems behave — and build tools that make them work better.
